Online primary care, also known as telemedicine or telehealth, is a way for patients to receive medical care remotely using electronic communication technology such as video conferencing or phone calls. Many general practitioners (GPs) offer online appointments to patients, allowing them to consult with a doctor from the comfort of their own home. This can be especially convenient for people who live in rural areas or who have mobility issues that make it difficult to visit a doctor in person. During an online appointment, a GP can diagnose and treat common medical conditions, prescribe medication, and offer advice and support to patients. Some GPs may also offer additional services such as referrals to specialists or follow-up care.

General Practice Department activities

Physical exams

GPs can perform physical exams to assess a patient's overall health and detect any potential health problems using telemedicine appointments, which can be conducted over the phone or through video conferencing. During a physical exam, a GP will assess a patient's overall health and check for any potential health problems. The GP may ask the patient about their medical history and current symptoms, and may also perform a variety of physical exams to check the patient's vital signs, such as their pulse, blood pressure, and temperature. The GP may also examine the patient's ears, nose, throat, and other parts of the body to check for any abnormalities.

Diagnosis and treatment of illnesses

GPs are trained to diagnose and treat common medical conditions such as colds and flu, allergies, and minor injuries. They can also prescribe medication if needed. GPs can also help patients manage chronic conditions such as diabetes, high blood pressure, and asthma. GPs can provide preventive care services such as vaccinations, screenings, and health counseling to help patients maintain good health. During an online appointment, a GP will ask about a patient's medical history, current symptoms, and any medications they are taking. The GP may also ask the patient to perform certain movements or provide other information that can help with diagnosis. Based on this information, the GP can diagnose and treat common medical conditions such as colds and flu, allergies, and minor injuries. The GP may also prescribe medication if needed.

Referrals to specialists

If a patient's condition requires further evaluation or treatment by a specialist, the GP can refer them to the appropriate healthcare provider. If the general practitioner (GP) determines that a patient's medical condition requires further evaluation or specialized treatment, they may refer the patient to a specialist. Specialists are healthcare providers who have advanced training in a specific area of medicine, such as cardiology, neurology, or oncology.

Mental health support

GPs can provide support for patients with mental health concerns, and can refer patients to mental health professionals if needed. Our Online GPs can provide mental health support to patients through telemedicine appointments, which can be conducted over the phone or through video conferencing. During an online appointment, a GP can assess a patient's mental health, diagnose any conditions, and recommend treatment options.
